Checkers Rules Explained: A Beginner's Guide

By Alexis Briggs on August 1, 2025

Checkers, also known as Draughts, is a classic board game enjoyed by people of all ages. Its simple rules belie a surprising amount of strategy, making it a game that’s both accessible and endlessly replayable. This guide will thoroughly explain the fundamental rules of checkers, equipping you with everything you need to start playing and understanding this timeless game.

Checkers Rules Explained: A Beginner's Guide

Checkers is a two-player game played on an 8x8 checkered board. Unlike chess, it’s a purely strategic game with no inherent advantage for either player. The goal is to capture all of your opponent’s pieces or block them so they have no legal moves. It’s a game of calculated risks, anticipating your opponent’s moves, and controlling key squares on the board.

Setting Up the Game

Before you begin, let's discuss the setup. The board is always set up with alternating dark and light squares. Each player starts with 12 pieces, placed on the dark squares of the first three rows closest to them. It’s crucial to understand that all pieces must begin on dark squares. Player 1 (traditionally red) occupies the first three rows, and Player 2 (traditionally black) occupies the last three rows.

How to Move

Each player takes turns moving one of their pieces per turn. Pieces can only move forward diagonally, one square at a time, onto an empty dark square. They cannot move backward unless they are “kinged” (explained below). Crucially, pieces can only move diagonally forward.

Movement is strictly controlled; you cannot jump over your own pieces, regardless of whether they are unoccupied squares.

Capturing Pieces

This is where the game becomes more dynamic. If an opponent’s piece is adjacent to one of your pieces, and the square immediately behind it is empty, you must jump over the opponent’s piece, capturing it. This capture is mandatory – you cannot choose to forgo a jump if one is available.

Once a piece is captured, it is immediately removed from the board. Jumps can be made in any direction, as long as the target square is empty. Multiple jumps can be made in a single turn if available – this is a key strategic element of the game.

A crucial rule to remember is that you must take a jump if one is available. This compulsory capture rule is what makes checkers a challenging and strategic game. Failure to adhere to this rule will result in a violation of the rules.

Becoming a King

When one of your pieces reaches the opposite end of the board (the last row on the opponent’s side), it becomes a “King.” This is achieved by placing a “King” marker (often a small coin or token) on the piece. A King possesses significantly increased mobility – it can now move forward and backward diagonally, one square at a time.

Kings are incredibly powerful in checkers. Their ability to move in both directions allows them to control more squares and capture opponent’s pieces from unexpected angles. The goal is often to get your pieces kinged to gain an advantage.

Endgame and Winning the Game

The game continues until one player has either captured all of their opponent’s pieces or blocked them so they have no legal moves. A player cannot win by simply forcing their opponent into a position where they have no moves; the game ends when all moves are impossible.

If both players are unable to make a move, the game is a draw. Determining the winner can be tricky, especially in the endgame. Strategic positioning and controlling the center of the board are often key to securing victory.

Summary of Key Rules

  1. Players move pieces diagonally forward, one square at a time.
  2. Mandatory jumps must be taken if available.
  3. Pieces can only jump over empty squares.
  4. Capturing is done by jumping over an opponent’s piece.
  5. When a piece reaches the opposite end of the board, it becomes a King, with increased mobility.
  6. The player with all of the opponent’s pieces wins, or if no moves are possible, the game is a draw.

With these rules firmly in mind, you’re well on your way to enjoying the fascinating world of checkers. Practice, strategy, and a little bit of luck will help you become a formidable opponent!
